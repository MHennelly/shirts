background:

  • a common way of encrypting text is to use hashing
  • hashing is when you give readable, plain text to a special function and it gives you back encrypted text
  • a "hash" is the encrypted text that comes out of the special function, hashes look like this
  • good hashing functions make their hashes impossible to reverse, if you could reverse SHA-512 hashes you'd get paid a lot (like a lot a lot) of money

how it works:

  • we create a hash with SHA-512 from a unique, secret passphrase
  • we mint the hash, this process gives it our signature so it stands apart from the fakes
  • we print a single shirt with this hash on it, we never re-print hashes
  • you get the t-shirt and passphrase
  • you are the only (and we mean only) person in the entire world who can hash our t-shirt

why it works:

  • $ echo -n "PASSPHRASE HERE" | sha512sum
  • ^ run that in a command line and your output will be what's printed on our shirt
  • anyone who makes a copy of your shirt will never be able to guess the right passphrase
  • bootleggers can always be spotted by asking them to successfully run that command (they can't because they don't know the passphrase)
  • as long as your passphrase is kept a secret, your shirt is 100% bootleg proof
