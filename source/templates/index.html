background info:

  • a common way of encrypting text is to use hashing
  • hashing is when you give readable, plain text to a special function like SHA-512
  • a "hash" is the encrypted text that comes out of the special function, hashes look like this
  • good hashing functions make their hashes virtually impossible to reverse

how it works:

  • we create an SHA-512 hash from a unique, secret passphrase
  • we mint the hash, this process gives it our signature so it stands apart from the fakes
  • we print a single shirt with this hash on it, we never print this hash again
  • you get the t-shirt and the passphrase
  • you are the only (and we mean only) person in the entire world who can hash our t-shirt

why it works:

  • $ echo -n "PASSPHRASE HERE" | sha512sum
  • ^ run that in a command line and your output will be what's printed on our shirt
  • imitators/scammers can always be spotted by asking them to successfully run that command
  • because imitators/scammers don't know the passphrase, they can never get that command to work
  • as long as the passphrase is kept a secret, your shirt will be the only legit one in the world with that hash
