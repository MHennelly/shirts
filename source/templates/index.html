background info:

  • Hashing Algorithms & Security
  • SHA: Secure Hashing Algorithm

the hashing command:

    read -s -p "Type Passphrase / Hit Enter: " X; echo "Hashing..."; for i in {1..10000}; do X=$(echo -n $X | sha512sum | tr -d ' -'); done; echo "Hash: $X";
    you can also try an in-browser JS example of the same algorithm here

how it works:

  • we generate a new hash, mint it, and print it on a t-shirt
  • we give you the t-shirt and the passphrase
  • you will be the only person in the entire world who can run the hashing command and get the t-shirt's hash
  • fakes can be spotted by asking the wearer to run that command successfully, they can't because they don't know the passphrase
  • as long as you keep the passphrase secret, your shirt will be the only legit copy ever

FAQ:

  • do you keep the passphrases?: no, never
  • why mint the hash?: this gives it our signature so real Moji hashes are publicly known
  • how do I run the command?: copy/paste it into the terminal application on your Linux or Mac computer and hit Enter
  • why hash 10,000 times?: this helps deter passphrase cracking by increasing the work needed to guess passphrases
